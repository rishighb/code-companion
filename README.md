# Code Companion  
*A UI/UX Design Project (Figma)*

## 📌 Project Overview

Code Companion is a UI/UX design project aimed at solving a real academic problem faced by Computer Science Engineering students at LPU.

Students often struggle to find updated lab experiments and viva resources because materials are scattered across WhatsApp groups, Google Drive folders, and outdated PDFs. Although experiments change every semester, they remain tied to core syllabus topics.

This project proposes a centralized, topic-based platform that links syllabus units with updated lab experiments and viva content.

---

## 🎯 Problem Statement

CSE students face difficulty accessing organized and updated lab experiments and viva materials. Existing resources are:

- Outdated  
- Scattered across multiple platforms  
- Not clearly aligned with syllabus topics  

There is a need for a structured, syllabus-mapped system that simplifies academic preparation.

---

## 🔎 Research Goals

- Understand how students currently access lab and viva resources  
- Identify pain points caused by outdated or unorganized content  
- Explore student expectations from a centralized platform  

### 🧠 Hypothesis

If lab experiments and viva questions are organized topic-wise on a centralized platform, students will:

- Save time  
- Reduce confusion  
- Improve exam readiness  

---

## 👥 Primary Research

- **Sample Size:** 30 Participants (10 from each year)  
- **Target Audience:** CSE students (2nd year to final year)  
- **Methods:** Online Surveys & In-Person Interviews  
- **Sampling Technique:** Stratified Purposive Sampling  

---

## 👤 User Personas

### Persona 1 – Nikith (3rd Year CSE Student)

- Finds lab manuals inconsistent with updated experiments  
- Cannot find viva questions in one place  
- Needs topic-wise, verified content  
- Feels overwhelmed during lab exams  

### Persona 2 – Rahul Varma (2nd Year CSE Student)

- Avoids relying on WhatsApp links  
- Prefers interactive and mobile-friendly content  
- Struggles when lab topics shift mid-semester  
- Wants structured, visual-first learning  

---

## 🧭 Customer Journey Mapping

Stages covered:

Awareness → Consideration → Usage → Resolution → Post-Use  

Key improvements identified:

- Clean onboarding  
- Topic filters and syllabus mapping  
- Bookmarking and contribution features  
- Gamification to encourage engagement  

---

## 🏗️ Information Architecture

The app is designed to:

- Organize experiments topic-wise  
- Allow syllabus-based filtering  
- Include viva question sections  
- Enable bookmarking and contributions
